LEARN HOW TO PROTECT YOUR FURNITURE WHEN MOVING

Protecting your possessions to ensure they reach the new house in the same state they left the old one is, without a doubt, the most important chore while packing for a move. For instance, properly packaging your furniture entails safeguarding your huge, priceless items against unintentional harm while transported.

Furniture items are notoriously difficult to pack for relocation because of their vast size and heavy weight. Many of the furniture you possess will be fairly delicate and fragile, with huge exterior surfaces that are particularly susceptible to dents, scratches, chips, and other sorts of damage. This will make things during your home move much more difficult than they need to be.

Furniture Pieces Inventory

Make a thorough inventory of every item of furniture you own, including beds, chairs, couches, nightstands, tables, desktops, dressers, closets, bookshelves, cabinets, and so on. Ensure to include all furniture since you’ll need that inventory list to pick which pieces to move to the new house and serve as a packing list later. When the relocation is finished, you may compare the delivered furniture to that inventory to confirm that you correctly transported every item. Go with dynamic movers that will help you.

Furniture Packing Preparation

    Ensure that you are thoroughly clean and dust your furniture before you start packing. During the moving process, dust and other particles can damage the furniture’s hard surfaces. In addition, ensuring the furniture is clean before moving will also enable you to avoid dirt in your new residence.

    Remove any pulls, knobs, or casters off your furniture. You should remove drawers fromdressers and desks so that you can placeother objects inside. This way, you’ll have space for storage and can easily move heavy furniture.

Dismantle Your Furniture

    It will be simpler to move huge, heavy furniture if those components are disassembled. It may be too challenging to carry as it’s too large to fit through the door.

    It may be safer to transport delicate furniture disassembled and with additional cushioning. Before you dismantle the furniture, be sure to keep to the manufacturer’s instructions for reconstructing it. Take pictures of each phase of the disassembling process to assist you in remembering which furniture components go where. Additionally, store screws and other tiny hardware in sealable plastic bags with clear labels.

Wrap Your Furniture

Knowing how to wrap furniture to move properly is crucial to prevent rips, scuffs, and even breakage.

  • Use bubble wrap and plastic sheets to cover fragile wood parts, such as legs, doors, or items like tables or dressers.

  • To safeguard any wooden furniture, use furniture blankets or moving blankets.

  • To provide additional protection and help avoid damages while the furniture is on the truck, you may place corrugated cardboard sheets between two pieces of furniture.

  • Wrap shelves and glass doors with packing paper to prevent damage.

  • To avoid scratches on a dresser, use a furniture blanket or a piece of cardboard when packing and wrapping it.

  • Glass tabletops ought to be put in telescopic cardboard boxes and wrapped with furniture blankets. You may always cover your tabletops with old blankets or towels if you have none.

  • Sofas and mattresses may be wrapped in specialized plastic coverings to protect them from moisture, tears, and stains. If you need the right coverings, you may wrap these things in plastic shrink wrap to keep them safe.

Correct Placement

    After the furniture has been thoroughly covered, adding it to the moving truck strategically increases security. To increase stability, place the bulkiest furnishings towards the back of the truck.

    Tables, dressers, and other types of square-shaped furniture should all have additional padding provided by moving blankets. The surfaces of the tables and dressers may be utilized to store extra goods once they have been put in. It would be best if you gave wrapped additional furniture security by being surrounded by layers of packing blankets

    Moving into a new home requires preparation to protect the furnishings. Make it clear to the movers where each piece of furniture should go. Less time spent fumbling for the right spot means less chance of furniture damage.
